Project Description

Q) What determines the quality of life , and which country has the best quality of life?

Safety should be one of the top parameters for a country's quality of life to be ranked higher - does the data support this assumption?

What about the purchasing power?
Does purchasing power go up or down when the quality of life improves? Or is there no direct co-relation between those two variables?

and finally, what about the cost of the living?
Are all the countries with the highest quality of living default to a higher cost of living? (** Since opportunities usually come at a price?). What if we had countries with the higher quality of life and still has a low cost of living? Would that be the most ideal scenario?

To answer these questions and more, we have followed a Martini structure to give just enough information for viewer to be interested in exploring on their own . here

Scenes

There are multiple scenes for the reader to navigate:
    ---> Quality of Life vs Cost of Living
    ---> Quality of Life vs Safety Index
    ---> Quality of Life vs Purchasing Power.
**For brevity, I will henceforth use the short form "QoL" to indicate "Quality of Life"

The first scene is the visual representation of QoL vs Cost of Living - the scatterplot pinpoints the data per country and also gives an overall trend.

There is clearly an upward trend : The Cost of living increases as the Quality of life improves in a country - this is in line with the very first conclusion in the Project Description section. How about the other two sections? Well, I don't want to give out spoilers - so please check those out here



Within all the scenes, we are using the following to ensure visual consistency amongst those scenes:

 a) All the scenes make use of the same scatterplot so that reader could focus on the core message rather than encountering a new chart type in every scene. This is done to keep the viewer oriented through the transactions and state changes.
b) All the scenes use the same theme - #B5C689 or ”Darkest Kelly”. (This background color was specifically chosen to be pleasing to the eye and not distract the viewer from the content)
c) All the scenes make use of same color template as well as the shape/size of the legends
d) Navigation between scenes is similar and easy to view --> hyperlinks are repeated where applicable for easy access as well as to illustrate the specific point.

Annotations

To represent the annotations within the scene, we can hover over the circle in the graph and see the exact country info along with the QoL index data.

The annotations are cleared within a scene when the cursor is moved away from the corresponding circle. Also, the annotations are cleared between scenes because we are using different parameters for displaying the scatterplot charts.

Parameters

Parameters are the variables used for narration - here the three variables are:

    Cost of Living,
    Safety Index     &
    Purchasing Power.

    The three buttons at the top introduce the three parameters for this visualization, namely "Qol vs Cost of Living", "Qol vs Safety Index" and "Qol vs Purchasing Power"
    Clicking on the buttons incorporates these parameters and triggers a change in the scenes. Please make note of the change in Y axis label and value - when button is clicked Thus clicking on these parameters controls the state of the visualization and gives the event state .

Triggers

Triggers are the actions that occur as a result of various interactions.
Here, there are numerous triggers which enable us to navigate between the home page and the visualization page as well as to the numerous sections within both of these pages.

The triggers are listed below:

1) On the home page, we have the menu on the left <-- with various sections such as 'Project Description' , 'Scenes', 'Annotations', 'Parameters', and 'Triggers'.
Clicking on any of these sections triggers a change in the display.
Thus all of these are triggers on the home page.

2) The link at the top titled "Skip this, take me to the actual Visualization" triggers another change in the page itself - this takes us to the visualization section.

3) Within the visualization section, the three buttons at the top for "Qol vs Cost of Living", "Qol vs Safety Index" and "Qol vs Purchasing Power" are the three triggers which cause an action change and the corresponding scatterplot chart is displayed.

NOTE: The user is made aware of the triggers by the use of interactive and prominent hyperlinks and buttons.
